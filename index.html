How do you know what's real?

Most people have no way to verify authenticity when they read news, download an app, or connect to WiFi. Meanwhile, machines decide what we can install, view, or share.

Consider this: that article might be AI-generated misinformation, your app store can delete your purchased software and content at any time, and your digital devices could contain backdoors.

We've outsourced trust to big tech companies who prioritize their control over our lives. Although we pay for it, we don't actually own our digital life.

truePASS: The stamp of real trust

PASS (Personal Authentic Software Services) is a simple process that enables instant verification of who created something and whether it's been modified.

0+pk+Ed25519(sk, sha512(pk+content))

For the technically curious, that's all there is to it. Just 97 bytes of cryptographic proof.
